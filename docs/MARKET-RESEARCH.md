# Market Research: High School Reunion Planning Platforms

*Research date: March 22, 2026*

---

## 1. Market Size & Opportunity

### The Numbers

- **~27,000 high schools** in the US (89% public, 11% private)
- **~3.9 million graduates** per year (2025 peak; projected to decline to ~3.4M by 2041)
- Reunions are typically held every **5 years** after graduation (5, 10, 15, 20, 25, 30... year milestones)
- That means roughly **5,400 graduating classes** are eligible for a reunion in any given year (27,000 schools / 5-year cycle)
- **20-30% of a class** typically attends a given reunion; ~40% attend at least one in their lifetime
- A "typical small reunion" costs **~$100/person**, with ~50 attendees = **~$5,000/event**

### Estimated Annual Market

- ~5,400 potential reunion events/year (conservative — many schools have multiple classes reuniting)
- Average spend: $5,000-$15,000 per event (varies widely by class size and ambition)
- **Estimated total market: $27M-$81M/year** in direct event spending
- Platform revenue opportunity (5-10% of event spend): **$1.4M-$8.1M/year**

*Note: Hard market size data for this niche is scarce. These are estimates based on available statistics.*

## 5. Gaps & Opportunities

### What's Missing from Existing Solutions

1. **No sponsorship tools** — None of the platforms have built-in sponsorship collection. Reunion committees handle this entirely manually (Facebook posts, personal asks, cash/Venmo).

2. **No digital yearbook/profile system** — ClassCreator has a classmate directory, but nobody offers a "what have you been up to?" questionnaire with browsable profiles and downloadable directory. This is the #1 thing people want at reunions and nobody's built it.

3. **Memorial pages are basic** — ClassCreator allows memorials but without a submission/review workflow. Most platforms have nothing.

4. **Multi-event support is weak** — Modern reunions are multi-day (Friday casual + Saturday formal). Most platforms treat it as one event with one ticket price.

5. **Dated UI/UX** — ClassCreator and AlumniClass have functional but visually dated interfaces. The space is ripe for a modern, mobile-first experience.

6. **Payment integration is fragmented** — Many platforms punt on payments (BYO PayPal/Venmo) or have complex fee structures. Integrated Stripe with transparent pricing is a differentiator.

7. **No "tease" / marketing mode** — Platforms assume you're ready to sell tickets. Nobody supports the months-long ramp-up of building excitement, collecting interest, and securing sponsors before opening registration.

### Common Complaints

- ClassCreator's Event Planner tool is worse than the older Reunion Planner it replaced
- PayPal integration issues across platforms (failed integrations, manual tracking)
- Eventbrite fees are too high for reunion budgets (~8-14% effective rate)
- Generic event platforms don't understand reunion-specific needs (classmate tracking, who's coming vs who's missing, memorials)
- Facebook Groups work for communication but can't handle payments, RSVPs, or structured data

## 7. Pricing Recommendation (If We Go SaaS)

Based on the competitive landscape:

### Proposed Tier Structure

| Tier | Price | Target | Includes |
|------|-------|--------|----------|
| **Free** | $0 | Small reunions, tryout | Basic site, up to 50 RSVPs, Stripe processing fees only |
| **Standard** | $29-49 one-time | Most reunions | Full site, unlimited RSVPs, sponsorship page, yearbook, memorial, up to 500MB storage |
| **Premium** | $79-99 one-time | Large/ambitious reunions | Everything in Standard + custom domain, priority support, 2GB storage, PDF yearbook generation |

### Why One-Time, Not Monthly

- Reunions are planned over 6-12 months, then done. Monthly pricing penalizes long planning cycles.
- ClassCreator charges $19-24/mo = $228-288/year. A one-time $49 fee undercuts significantly.
- AlumniClass is free (but dated). Our free tier competes here.
- Transaction fees (Stripe's 2.9% + 30¢) provide ongoing revenue without a subscription.

### Revenue Modeling (Speculative)

- If 1% of the ~5,400 annual reunion-eligible classes use the platform = **54 customers/year**
- At $49 average: **$2,646/year** from platform fees
- At $100 average ticket × 50 attendees × 2.9% Stripe passthrough: **$7,830/year** in processing volume
- This is a **lifestyle business** at small scale, not a VC-scale opportunity
- At 10% market penetration (540 reunions/year): ~$26K platform + ~$78K processing volume

### The Real Opportunity

The platform itself is a low-revenue business. The real value is:
1. **Proving the model** with one reunion (PHHS '96)
2. **Building a reusable product** that can be offered free/cheap to build a user base
3. **Potential pivot** to broader alumni engagement (not just reunions) or event planning
4. **Portfolio piece** demonstrating full-stack product development
